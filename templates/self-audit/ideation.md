# Ideation — the non-obvious ways to make it dramatically better

The generative lens. Deliberately the OPPOSITE of an audit: it licenses invention,
speculation, and disagreement with the current design. Run it as a **follow-up in
the same session** after `system-audit.md` or `tooling-audit.md` completes — ideas
grounded in "I just read your whole system" beat cold brainstorming. See `README.md`.

Reusable on any subject (a template, a product direction), not just a governance
system.

```
Now switch modes completely. The audit is done — forget "find what's broken."
This is open-ended ideation, and I'm giving you explicit license the audit denied
you: invent, speculate, disagree with how this system is currently designed,
propose things I never asked for. I want the ideas I would never think of myself.

You've just read the whole system. Step back and think about what it could BECOME,
not just how to patch it.

Rules for your thinking:
- Your first three ideas will be the obvious ones (add tags, add a dashboard,
  automate the sweep). State them in one line each and then PUSH PAST them — the
  value is in ideas 4 through 15.
- Challenge the premise, don't just optimize within it. Is this system even
  solving the right problem? If you were designing "how a fleet of AI sessions
  keeps a shared brain current" from a blank page today, what would it look like —
  and what's the cheapest path from where I am to there?
- Give me SUBTRACTION ideas, not just additions. What should this system STOP
  doing, delete, or radically simplify? What complexity is it carrying that isn't
  earning its keep? (Best ideas often live here.)
- Steal from other domains. What do compilers, immune systems, newsrooms, open-
  source governance, spaced-repetition, or supply chains do that this system could
  borrow? Name the analogy and the transplant.
- Think at 10x scale. When many more AI sessions and repos hit this system, what
  breaks, and what design would make scale an asset instead of a liability?
- Find the second-order effects. What is this system quietly teaching the humans
  and the sessions to do — good or bad — that no one intended?

Output as two clearly separated tiers:
  TIER 1 — HIGH CONVICTION: ideas you'd bet on, with a cheap first step.
  TIER 2 — BOLD / SPECULATIVE: things that might be wrong but would be huge if
  right. Do not self-censor these down into safe ideas — I want the swings.

For each idea give: (a) the one-line pitch, (b) the non-obvious INSIGHT behind it
(the thing I probably haven't seen), (c) rough effort, (d) what it unlocks. Rank
within each tier by leverage. End with the ONE idea you'd chase first if this were
your system, and why.
```
